# What Is IML (In-Mould Labelling) and Why Are FMCG Brands Switching to It?

**IML (In-Mould Labelling) is a decoration process where a pre-printed plastic label is placed inside the injection mould before the container is formed — the molten plastic fuses with the label during moulding, creating a container where the label is permanently bonded into the surface. FMCG brands are adopting it because it delivers premium shelf aesthetics, eliminates label peel and moisture lift, and is compatible with PP recycling streams.**

---

## How Does IML Work?

The IML process has four stages:

1. **Label preparation:** A pre-printed PP or BOPP film label is die-cut to the exact container surface shape and electrostatically charged
2. **Label placement:** A robotic arm places the charged label inside the open mould cavity, where it adheres to the mould wall by electrostatic force
3. **Injection moulding:** Molten PPCP is injected into the mould, encapsulating the back of the label and fusing it into the container wall as it cools
4. **Demoulding:** The finished container is ejected with the label permanently integrated into the surface — no adhesive, no separate labelling line

The result is a container where the label and body are effectively one piece.

## Why Are FMCG Brands Choosing IML?

**Shelf presence and brand equity.** IML delivers photographic-quality, full-surface decoration with no label edges, bubbles, or overlap seams. On a retail shelf, IML containers are visually indistinguishable from premium European imports — and consumers notice.

**Moisture and cold-chain durability.** Self-adhesive labels routinely fail in refrigerated display (condensation lifts the adhesive) and in humid conditions. IML containers used for ghee, dairy spreads, sauces, and beverages retain perfect label appearance throughout their entire shelf life.

**Operational efficiency.** IML eliminates a separate labelling line, reducing floor space, labour, and label inventory management. The decoration and moulding happen in one step.

**Sustainability alignment.** Because both the container and IML label are PP (polypropylene), the container is mono-material — easier to recycle than a PP container with a PET or metalised label. This supports EPR compliance and on-pack recyclability claims.

## What Products Are Suited to IML Containers?

IML is most commercially justified for products with high retail visibility, significant volumes, and brand differentiation needs:

- Ghee, butter, and dairy spreads (premium tubs)
- Ready-to-eat foods, condiments, and dips
- Personal care (creams, lotions, hair care)
- Premium paint and coating brands (decorative product lines)
- Lubricant brands targeting retail automotive aftermarket

For plain industrial containers (paint, chemicals, lubricants in trade packs), self-adhesive labels remain practical and cost-effective. IML earns its premium when brand appearance drives purchase decisions.

## What Is the Investment Required for IML?

IML requires a higher upfront investment than standard labelling:

- **IML-compatible injection mould** with label placement features
- **IML robot** integrated with the injection moulding machine
- **Label printing and die-cutting** (typically outsourced to specialist label converters)

These investments are justified at annual volumes of 300,000+ units per SKU, where the per-unit savings from eliminating a labelling line and the brand value premium offset the tooling and label cost differential.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. What does IML stand for in plastic packaging?**
IML stands for In-Mould Labelling. It is a container decoration process where a pre-printed plastic label is permanently fused into the container surface during the injection moulding process, eliminating the need for a separate labelling step and creating a label that cannot peel, lift, or bubble.

**Q2. Is IML more expensive than self-adhesive labelling?**
The per-unit cost of an IML container is higher than a plain container with a self-adhesive label when comparing production cost alone. However, IML eliminates the labelling line (capital, labour, and maintenance), reduces label waste, and improves line efficiency. The total cost of ownership is competitive at volumes above approximately 300,000 units per SKU annually.

**Q3. Can IML containers be recycled?**
Yes. When the IML label is made from PP film (the same material as the container), the decorated container is effectively mono-material polypropylene and can enter the PP (#5) recycling stream without contamination. This is a significant recyclability advantage over containers labelled with PET or metalised film labels.

**Q4. What print quality is achievable with IML?**
IML labels are printed using offset, gravure, or digital printing on high-quality BOPP or PP film, achieving photographic-quality results with sharp colour reproduction, fine detail, and consistent registration. The moulding process does not degrade print quality — the finished container typically looks better than any other labelling method because there are no surface texture discontinuities or edge effects.
