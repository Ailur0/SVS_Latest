# What Is the Minimum Order Quantity for Custom Plastic Containers in India?

**For standard-size PPCP containers using existing moulds, minimum order quantities (MOQ) in India typically start at 500–1,000 units per SKU. For custom sizes requiring new mould development, MOQs are higher — usually 2,000–5,000 units — to recover tooling investment, though this varies by manufacturer and container complexity.**

---

## Why Does MOQ Exist for Plastic Containers?

MOQ is set primarily by two cost factors:

**Machine setup and run cost.** Injection moulding machines require time and material to set up, purge, and reach stable production conditions. Below a certain quantity, the setup cost per unit becomes economically unviable for the manufacturer.

**Tooling amortisation.** For custom containers, a steel mould must be fabricated — a cost that ranges from ₹1.5 lakh to ₹15 lakh or more depending on cavity count, container size, and complexity. This cost is amortised across the order quantity, meaning low-volume orders carry a disproportionate tooling burden per unit.

---

## What Are Typical MOQs by Container Type?

| Container Type | Mould Status | Typical MOQ |
|---|---|---|
| Standard 5L paint bucket | Existing mould | 500–1,000 units |
| Standard 10L or 20L container | Existing mould | 300–700 units |
| Custom size (new mould required) | New mould | 2,000–5,000 units |
| Custom shape + IML decoration | New mould + IML setup | 3,000–10,000 units |
| Multi-cavity mould (high volume SKU) | New mould | 5,000–25,000 units |

These are indicative ranges. Final MOQ depends on the specific manufacturer's capacity, mould ownership, and your negotiated commercial terms.

---

## How Can You Reduce the Effective MOQ?

**Use an existing standard mould.** If your product can be packaged in a standard container size (1L, 5L, 10L, 20L), you avoid tooling cost entirely and benefit from the manufacturer's existing lower MOQ thresholds.

**Share tooling cost upfront.** Some manufacturers will lower the MOQ if you contribute partially to the mould cost, effectively buying dedicated capacity.

**Consolidate SKUs.** If you have multiple products that can share the same container shape, consolidating orders across SKUs can help you meet volume thresholds more easily.

**Negotiate annual volume commitments.** Manufacturers are often willing to lower per-order MOQ if you commit to an annual volume purchase agreement — the risk reduction justifies the flexibility.

## What Information Do You Need to Get an Accurate MOQ Quote?

To receive a precise MOQ and unit price quotation, provide your manufacturer with:

- Container volume and dimensions (or reference an existing standard size)
- Neck finish / closure type required
- Colour (natural, white, or custom colour)
- Labelling method (self-adhesive label, shrink sleeve, or IML)
- Material requirement (standard PPCP or food-grade / chemical-grade specification)
- Delivery frequency and lead time expectations

The more detail you provide upfront, the more accurate and committed the MOQ and pricing will be.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I order a sample before committing to MOQ?**
Yes. Most Indian container manufacturers offer pre-production samples from existing moulds at a nominal cost. For custom moulds, a sample (T1 trial shot) is typically provided after mould fabrication and before full production approval. Discuss sample requirements and costs during the quotation stage.

**Q2. Who owns the mould — the buyer or the manufacturer?**
This is negotiable. If the buyer pays for mould fabrication, they typically own the mould and can move production to another manufacturer. If the manufacturer bears the tooling cost, they retain ownership and the buyer is tied to that supplier for the container. Clarify mould ownership in writing before proceeding.

**Q3. How long does it take to develop a new custom mould in India?**
Standard injection moulds for containers take 6–12 weeks from design approval to first trial samples, depending on complexity and the manufacturer's fabrication capacity. Complex multi-cavity moulds or containers with undercuts and integrated handles may take 12–16 weeks.

**Q4. What is the price difference between standard and custom containers?**
Standard containers from existing moulds are significantly cheaper per unit — often 20–40% lower — because there is no tooling cost to amortise. Custom containers justify the premium when your product requires a specific shape, closure, or capacity that standard sizes cannot provide, or when branded differentiation is a commercial priority.
